Powerplay System

The Powerplay is Split/Second's signature mechanic: you charge a three-tier Power bar by driving aggressively, then spend it to detonate the environment — fuel trucks, collapsing buildings, exploding planes, derailing trains — under rival cars. This page documents the actual runtime economy, the dynamic trigger / "danger zone" targeting system, the trigger-button mapping, the camera director that frames each detonation, and the per-level data that ships in the devkit build. Almost everything here is read straight out of plaintext .params files and XML, so most facts carry a Confirmed badge.

Architecture at a glance

The system is split across a handful of classes named in the EBOOT strings. The cPowerplayManager owns the loaded set of powerplays for the current subtrack; the per-player cPowerplayController drives charging and triggering; the cPowerplayMaterialManager handles the on-track highlight shading that paints a chargeable object in the player's available colour; and the PowerplaySequenceDirector (a sub-component of the Camera Director) frames the cinematic shots.

Class / componentRole
cPowerplayManagerLoads & owns the level's powerplay set ("new" / "load" passes seen in EBOOT). Inputs/outputs via cPowerplayManagerInput / cPowerplayManagerOutput.
cPowerplayControllerPer-player power-bar charging, trigger eligibility, firing.
cPowerplayMaterialManagerHighlight material shading (Level 1/2/3 Colour A & B).
cPowerplayInfoRuntime record for one placed powerplay (matches powerplayInfo.dat).
cPowerplayTriggeredEvent / cPowerplayCrashEventTelemetry / replay events recorded by TrackTivity.
PowerplaySequenceDirectorCinematic camera sequencing for the detonation.
cMegabowlesControllerAutomated powerplay-scenario test/profiler harness ("Megabowles").
The "Powerplay Controller" also appears in the EBOOT alongside the other top-level game controllers (HUD Controller, Audio Controller, Game Logic Controller, Map Maker, Environment Controller), confirming it is a first-class subsystem rather than a script.

The Power bar economy

The bar is a 0–100 percentage. Three activation thresholds carve it into tiers; spending a tier fires powerplays of the matching Power Level. The thresholds and penalties live in each game mode's RaceSetup/Powerbar block (e.g. GameLogic/GameModes/ChampRace.params):

/RaceSetup/Powerbar:
    'Power gained on auto-respot'                  = 0.0  (-100.0, 100.0);
    'Power required to activate level 1 (percentage)' = 33  (0, 100);
    'Power required to activate level 2(percentage)'  = 66  (0, 100);
    'Power required to activate level 3(percentage)'  = 100 (0, 100);
    'Wreck penalty'                                = 33.0 (0.0, 100.0);
TierPower requiredTypical use
Level 133%Small/medium hazards — barrels, doors, small explosions, shortcut openers.
Level 266%(Threshold present; the shipping powerplay data uses mostly L1 and L3 — see below.)
Level 3100%Route Changers — collapsing buildings, derailed trains, plane crashes; reshape the track.

All three tiers can be individually toggled per mode via RaceSetup/Powerplays (Level 1/2/3 powerplays enabled, plus the master Powerplay system enabled). The bar drains by the Wreck penalty (33.0, i.e. one full tier) when you crash. A debug cheat RaceSetupStatic/Logic/PowerBar/Enable Cheat = true exists in this QA build (debug actions DebugCheat_FullPowerbar / DebugCheat_AIFullPowerbar).

How power is earned

Power is awarded by the scoring system (RaceSetup/Scoring) for a set of skilful actions, each with min/max per-second rates. Real ChampRace values:

ActionPower/sec (min → max)Conditions
Drifting7.0 → 14.0Angle 10°–90°, 50–100 mph; overtake bonus +8.0
Jumping5.0 → 10.050–100 mph; combos enabled; overtake +8.0
Drafting2.0 → 4.0Within a 10° half-cone, 0–45 m behind, ≥100 mph, ≥0.2 s
Near Miss (environmental)10.0 (award)≥70 mph, ≥0.1 s in box, 0.5 s cooldown
Speed0.0 (mode-tuned)≥90 mph; scaled by position
Victim / Nudge16.67 / 66.67 (award)Power for being crashed into / nudging a rival into a wreck

Two global multipliers in RaceSetup/Scoring reward duration and underdogs: Power scale added every second of event duration = 0.05 and Power scale for last position = 1.15. The AI earns power through its own simplified table (GameplayParameters/AI Powerbar): Drifting 14.0/s, Jump 10.0/s (after 0.5 s airborne), Drafting 4.0/s (≥5 m, ≤5° off the player), Drive-Fast 1.0/s (above 60% top speed).

Powerplay levels & route changers

Each placed powerplay has a PowerLevel (1–3) and flags such as RouteChanging and OpenShortcut. Level 3 route changers permanently reshape the lap — they set RouteChanging="true" and may flag TotallyUndriveable for the old path. In the shipping Downtown subtrack A data, the 111 placed powerplays break down as 26 × Level 1 and 85 × Level 3 — the bulk of a level is small chargeable hazards plus a handful of dramatic route changers.

RouteChangers / EnabledLevel-altering Level 3 powerplays.

GameEventsScripted powerplays for race events: PGE_Start_P, PGE_Lap_P, PGE_End_P, PA13_StartGloryMovie_P (intro).

The dynamic trigger system & danger zones

Split/Second shipped a rewritten targeting model: in GameLogic/GameplayParameters.params the flag Use the new dynamic trigger system implementation = true selects it. Rather than firing on a fixed line, each powerplay carries a danger zone — an oriented box (the victimMtx transform + victimExtent in the .powerplays file) that defines where a rival must be for the hit to count.

/GameplayParameters/Powerplays/Dynamic Trigger:
    '000;Use the new dynamic trigger system implementation' = true;
    '001;Extra extent to add on the danger zones'           = 0.0 (0.0, 50.0);

/GameplayParameters/Powerplays:
    '000;Allow trigger even if own player could be a victim' = true;

The static side adds RaceSetupStatic/Logic/SelfFiringPowerPlay/Target Danger Zone Time = 0.35 — how far ahead, in seconds, the system projects a rival into the zone — and RaceSetupStatic/Logic/TriggeredPowerPlay/Allow rear-firing = false. The runtime debug-render menu exposes the geometry directly:

powerplays/Render/Victim Boxes
powerplays/Render/Danger Zones
powerplays/Render/Enabled Trigger Boxes
powerplays/Render/Disabled Trigger Boxes
powerplays/Render/Powerplay Age
A single disable_danger_zone flag on an AreaOfEffect turns a detonation into pure spectacle that can't kill — used by the "NonFatalExplosion", "Wakeup_physics_props" and "depth_charge" effect profiles.

Triggered vs. self-firing

Two runtime components consume the danger-zone data. RaceSetup/Logic/TriggeredPowerPlay (Enabled = true) handles the normal "fire it on the car behind/ahead of you" case and does a wrong-way check (Do Wrong Way Check = true). RaceSetup/Logic/SelfFiringPowerPlay (disabled in ChampRace) lets the game auto-trigger powerplays on the holder — used by special modes and tutorials, gated by Min/Max % Of Power To Self Trigger.

Trigger buttons

Powerplays are bound to face buttons through a small tweakable group the EBOOT formats as Powerplays/TriggerButtons/%s (and %s_%02d for indexed variants). Each button entry carries:

FieldNotes
a; Trigger IndexWhich powerplay slot this button fires.
b; Reset TriggerRe-arm / reset behaviour.

The HUD shows the available tier's icon (from GameLogic/PowerplayIcons.textures / UI/HUD/PowerPlayIcons.gfx) and prints prompt messages when RaceSetup/HUD/Powerplay Trigger Messages Enabled = true.

Per-level powerplay assets

Every drivable subtrack ships a .powerplays XML file plus a binary powerplayInfo.dat, Powerplay cameras, and a draw-distance table. Layout under Environments/Levels/<Level>/:

FileFormatContents
Subtracks/<A-D>/<Level>.powerplaysXMLPlaced powerplay instances (per subtrack route).
<Level>_powerplayInfo.datBinary, big-endianRuntime table of .cat timeline refs + transforms + extents.
Cameras/PowerplayCameras.xmlXMLList of powerplays that own a bespoke camera.
Cameras/PowerplayCameraSubjects.xmlXMLCamera subjects (animation/bone targets).
Params/PowerPlayDrawDists.params.paramsPer-asset fade range, max draw distance, bounding-box render.

The devkit carries powerplay data for ten levels: Downtown, Graveyard, Powerplant, docks, airport_test_03 and the Nemesis-mode variants nem_downtown, nem_graveyard, nem_storm, nem_training, nem_warehouse. Shared powerplay definitions and timelines live in the top-level Powerplays/ folder (Animations, Timelines, VehicleTimelines).

powerplayInfo.dat (binary twin)

The .dat is the cooked, big-endian runtime form: a record per powerplay holding a Catnip .cat timeline name, a transform, and bounds. The first bytes of Downtown_powerplayInfo.dat:

000000  00 00 00 58 80 73 33 3c  00 00 00 19 70 70 31 32  ...X.s3<....pp12
000010  5f 72 6f 61 64 5f 65 78  70 6c 6f 64 65 5f 6d 70  _road_explode_mp
000020  6f 2e 63 61 74 3f 7f ff  ff 00 00 00 00 3a 19 0d  o.cat?.......:..

You can read 00 00 00 19 (25) as the length of the string pp12_road_explode_mpo.cat that follows, then big-endian floats (3f 7f ff ff ≈ 0.99998) for the transform — see Powerplay Info (.dat) for the full layout.

Timelines & the link to Area of Effects

What a powerplay actually does is a list of timeline XMLs (Powerplays/Timelines/*_T.xml, 106 of them in the build). A timeline sequences particle .cat effects, animations, audio and — critically — an AreaOfEffect that applies forces to nearby cars. Excerpt from CarExplode_1_T.xml:

<TimeLine TimeLine_Total_Time="5.0" TimeLine_Number_Effects="2">
  <TimeLine_Effect Effect_Name="AreaOfEffect" Effect_Type="9"
     area_of_effect_type="Standard"
     camera_shake_scale="1.0" handling_impulse_scale="1.0"
     crash_radius_proportion="0.5" disable_danger_zone="false"
     allow_self_kills="false" allow_player_kills="false"> ... </TimeLine_Effect>
  <TimeLine_Effect Effect_Name="...\EXP_FireBall_L.cat" Effect_Type="0"> ... </TimeLine_Effect>
</TimeLine>

The area_of_effect_type attribute names one of the profiles defined in AreaOfEffects/EffectParams.params (Standard, Crash, NonFatalExplosion, Wakeup_physics_props, depth_charge…). Across the 106 timelines the AoE types break down as 14 × Standard, 11 × custom_type, 1 × Wakeup_physics_props. The full force model is documented on the Area of Effects & Handling page.

Powerplay Sequence Director & cameras

When a powerplay fires, the Camera Director hands off to its PowerplaySequenceDirector, which plays a multi-shot cinematic. Its tweakables (EBOOT strings under CameraDirector/PowerplaySequenceDirector):

TweakRole
Sequence LengthTotal cinematic duration.
Powerplay Age To Rewind TimeHow old a powerplay can be and still get a "rewind" shot.
Loop Sequence / Lock Start TimesSequence looping / fixed shot timing.
Intro Camera + Intro Camera Shot LengthOpening shot.
Shot%d (Enabled, Start Time, Transition Length, Non Linear Transition, Transition Effect Name, Time Offset)Per-shot configuration of the sequence.

Whether a takedown cinematic plays is gated by a set of camera conditions (CameraDirector/CameraConditions/*): "Use Drifting Or Jumping Condition", "Use Min Time Between Takedowns Condition", "Use Can Trigger Other Powerplay Condition", "Use Victim To Close Condition", "Use Victim Is In Loaded Sector Condition", "Use Victim Powerplay Is Most Recent Condition". A global CameraDirector/Min Time Between Takeout Cameras rate-limits them, and game_setup/min_distance_to_victim_for_takedown sets a minimum range. Named powerplay camera styles include Powerplay_Trackside, Powerplay_Detonation, Powerplay_Scrolling and the Powerplay_Sequence1_Shot%d chain.

Per level, PowerplayCameras.xml lists which powerplays own a bespoke camera (e.g. Downtown: PA01_Collapsing_Building_P, PP10_PierExplode_Bridge, RC02_Train_Derailment_P, PP08_SkyScraper_P…), and PowerplayCameraSubjects.xml binds each to an AnimationName / BoneName subject to track.

Highlight materials

Chargeable objects glow in the player's available colour via the cPowerplayMaterialManager. The colours are tweaks Powerplays/Highlight/Level 1–3 - Colour A and … - Colour B (an A/B pair per tier), selected by the powerplay's MasterMaterial id. Draw distances and bounding-box debug rendering per asset come from Params/PowerPlayDrawDists.params (Default Draw Dist, Fade Range, Max Draw Dist, Render Bounding Box).

Testing: the Megabowles harness

Black Rock automated powerplay QA with cMegabowlesController ("Megabowles"), a scenario test/profiler that drives cars through powerplays repeatedly. The Camera Director also exposes PowerplaySequenceDirector/Debug Takedown Sequences and CameraDirector/Debug/GameEventSequences/{Intro,Start,Finish,Outro} for shot review.

Power-level usage in the shipping data is uneven: Downtown subtrack A uses only Level 1 and Level 3 powerplays (no Level 2 placements), even though the 66% threshold exists. Whether Level 2 is exercised on other tracks/modes is an open question for a later pass.
