Area of Effects & Handling

When a powerplay or scripted explosion goes off, the thing that actually shoves cars around is an Area of Effect (AoE). The AoE system applies physics impulses (Roll, Slowing, Spin) and timed handling penalties (loss of steering, powerslide, camera shake) to every car inside its radius — and crucially, it applies different effects depending on who you are relative to the blast: the player who fired it, the intended victim, an AI car, or an AI hit by another AI. All of it lives in three plaintext .params files under Deferred/AreaOfEffects/, so this page is almost entirely Confirmed with real numbers.

Global parameters — the base force

GlobalParams.params defines the baseline velocity/falloff an AoE imparts before any per-effect scaling. Verbatim:

/AreaOfEffects/GlobalParameters:
    'a; Min Car Distance Factor'        = 0.75 (0.0, 1.0);
    'b; Standard Velocity'              = 20.0 (0.0, 100.0);
    'c; Standard Angular Velocity '     = 10.0 (0.0, 100.0);
    'd; Standard Min force proportion ' = 0.2  (0.0, 1.0);
    'e; Standard Up velocity multiplier ' = 1.0 (0.0, 10.0);
ParamValueMeaning (interpretation)
Min Car Distance Factor0.75Fraction of the radius below which a car is treated as "at the centre" for full force.
Standard Velocity20.0Base linear velocity (m/s) imparted at full strength.
Standard Angular Velocity10.0Base spin imparted at full strength.
Standard Min force proportion0.2Floor — a car at the edge of the radius still gets ≥20% of the force.
Standard Up velocity multiplier1.0Scales the upward (loft) component of the blast.
The "Min force proportion" of 0.2 means the falloff never reaches zero inside the radius — even a glancing blast nudges you, which is why AoE hits feel decisive rather than fizzling at the edges. Theory on the exact falloff curve; the magnitudes themselves are Confirmed.

The three impulse axes

At the bottom of HandlingEffects.params, the /AreaOfEffects/Handling group defines the three signed impulse magnitudes an AoE can apply, plus debug routing:

/AreaOfEffects/Handling:
    'Test Forces on self temp hack' = false;
    'a;Force Type'                  = false;
    'b;Forced Effect Type'          = 'On Opponent';
    'c; Vehicle Roll Impulse'    = 2000.0  (-10000.0, 10000.0);
    'd; Vehicle Slowing Impulse' = 2000.0  (-50000.0, 50000.0);
    'e; Vehicle Spin Impulse'    = 1500.0  (-50000.0, 50000.0);
ImpulseValueRangeEffect on the car
Vehicle Roll Impulse2000.0±10000Rolls / tips the car about its forward axis.
Vehicle Slowing Impulse2000.0±50000Kills forward momentum (the "shove backwards").
Vehicle Spin Impulse1500.0±50000Yaws / spins the car about its vertical axis.

The debug fields above let QA force every AoE to behave as one target type: Force Type = true plus Forced Effect Type = 'On Opponent' (other valid values map to the four profiles below), and Test Forces on self temp hack redirects the force onto the local car. Both are false in the shipping data.

Per-target handling effects

The same explosion is felt differently by different cars. HandlingEffects.params defines four handling-effect profiles, one per relationship to the blast. Each is a timed loss of control plus a crash-resistance factor:

/AreaOfEffects/Handling/HandlingEffect/Self Effect:
    'Camera Shake Scale'      = 1.0  (0.0, 10.0);
    'Crash Resistance Factor' = 0.5  (0.0, 1.0);
    'Powerslide Time'         = 0.0  (0.0, 10.0);
    'Steering Scale'          = 0.0  (0.0, 10.0);
    'Steering Time'           = 0.0  (0.0, 10.0);

Reading the table:

  • Crash Resistance Factor — higher = harder to wreck. The AI is tough when a human hits it (0.85) but fragile when another AI hits it (0.2), which keeps the cinematic carnage flowing in the pack without making the human's own kills feel cheap.
  • Steering Scale / Steering Time are zero for both human profiles (the human keeps steering control), but non-zero for AI (Scale 1.0, Time 3.0 s for player-on-AI) — the AI's steering is deliberately wrenched so it visibly fishtails.
  • Powerslide Time throws the target into a slide: 0.5 s for a human victim, up to 5.0 s for an AI struck by the player.
  • Camera Shake only matters for the camera-owning human cars (1.0); AI profiles set it to 0.
"Self Effect" exists because you can be caught in your own blast — the firer still gets shake (1.0) and a 0.5 crash-resistance, but keeps full steering. Whether you can actually wreck yourself is separately gated by allow_self_kills (see effect profiles below) and the mode flag RaceSetup/Powerplays/Allow trigger even if own player could be a victim.

Effect-type profiles (recipes)

EffectParams.params defines seven named effect types. Each is a set of 0–1 proportions and scales that say "for this kind of detonation, how much of each subsystem do we apply?" — camera, handling slip/impulse, fire, props, crash — plus two booleans. A timeline's AreaOfEffect effect names one of these via area_of_effect_type="…", then can override any field inline.

FieldControls
camera_radius_proportion / camera_time_proportionHow much of the camera-effect radius / duration this type uses.
camera_shake_scale / camera_shake_timeShake intensity / duration (seconds).
handling_slip_radius_proportion / …_time_proportion / handling_slip_scaleReach, duration and strength of the loss-of-grip effect.
handling_impulse_scaleMultiplier on the Roll/Slowing/Spin impulses above.
fire_radius_proportion / fire_time_proportion / set_car_on_fire_timeFire reach/duration and how long a hit car burns.
prop_radius_proportion / prop_time_proportion / hit_props_scaleReach/duration/strength applied to physics props (debris, barriers).
crash_radius_proportion / crash_time_proportionReach/window in which the blast can force a wreck.
allow_self_killsMay the firer be wrecked by it. (All ship false.)
disable_danger_zoneMake the effect non-lethal spectacle (skips victim targeting).

The pattern is clear: Crash is the maximal lethal blast (full crash window), Standard tones the crash window down to 0.6 radius / 0.5 time so it still kills but with a tighter sweet spot, and the rest are deliberately defanged — Visual_effects_only keeps the camera shake but zeroes handling, crash and props; Wakeup_physics_props / depth_charge only nudge nearby physics props (hit_props_scale 0.01 / 0.65) and switch the danger zone off entirely. Note that NonFatalExplosion, Wakeup_physics_props and depth_charge are the three profiles that set disable_danger_zone = true, and every profile ships with allow_self_kills = false.

How a timeline invokes an AoE

The link between a powerplay and these numbers is the timeline. Inside a Powerplays/Timelines/*_T.xml, an effect with Effect_Name="AreaOfEffect" and Effect_Type="9" names a type and may override individual fields. From CarExplode_1_T.xml:

<TimeLine_Effect Effect_Name="AreaOfEffect" Effect_Type="9"
   area_of_effect_type="Standard"
   camera_shake_scale="1.0" camera_shake_time="2.0"
   handling_slip_radius_proportion="1.0" handling_impulse_scale="1.0"
   crash_radius_proportion="0.5" crash_time_proportion="0.0"
   track_damage_radius_proportion="1.0" track_damage_intensity="1.0"
   allow_self_kills="false" disable_danger_zone="false" allow_player_kills="false">
  <Effect_Transform> <Row0 X="50" .../> ... </Effect_Transform>
  <Effect_Timer Timer_Start="0.0" Timer_End="2.04" Effect_Stretch="1.0"/>
</TimeLine_Effect>

Three things to notice: the Effect_Transform scales the AoE box (here 50 units on each axis — the radius), the Effect_Timer gives the active window (0 → 2.04 s), and the timeline adds fields not in the base profile — track_damage_* (cosmetic track scorching) and allow_player_kills. Across the 106 timelines in the build the AoE type usage is 14 × Standard, 11 × custom_type (fully inline-tuned), 1 × Wakeup_physics_props.

The crash decision & intensity

Being inside the crash radius/time window doesn't always wreck you — the game mode decides. RaceSetup/Powerplays/AreaOfEffect in each mode carries Instant crashes enabled (true in ChampRace) and an Always kill override. For modes using accumulated damage, an intensity model is provided:

/RaceSetup/Logic/AreaOfEffectKills:
    Enabled                  = false;
    IntensityPerAOEHit       = 0.6  (0.0, 2.0);
    IntensityDecayRate       = 0.5  (0.0, 5.0);
    MaxIntensityBeforeCrash  = 2.0  (0.0, 10.0);

Here each AoE hit adds 0.6 intensity, decaying at 0.5/s; reaching 2.0 forces the crash — so ~4 quick hits, or fewer if they land in a burst. The post-crash recovery is driven by RaceSetupStatic/Logic/Victim (Area of Effect Crash Timer = 3.0 s) and the mode's RaceSetup/WreckSequence (Wreck time 3.0 s, AI 2.0 s, post-crash rolling restart 70 mph). The Respot / Catchup system then re-introduces wrecked cars.

Several damage paths feed the same crash bookkeeping — Hitbox Crash Timer (direct vehicle contact) vs. Area of Effect Crash Timer (blast) are tracked separately (both 3.0 s in this build). The exact priority when both fire in the same frame is not pinned down here. Partial

Where the targets come from

Which of the four handling profiles a given car receives is decided by the powerplay controller from the firer/victim relationship and the danger-zone test, not by the AoE data itself. The AoE files supply the magnitudes; the powerplay system supplies the routing (Self / Opponent / AI / AI-on-AI) and whether the danger zone is even consulted (disable_danger_zone). The two systems are therefore best read together — see the Powerplay System page for the targeting and trigger side.
