Difficulty & Race Director

Split/Second keeps the AI pack tight and dramatic through a layer of "race director" systems that sit on top of the raw SSAI driving model. A DifficultyModifiers preset shapes how skilled each grid slot is and how aggressively they trigger Powerplays; a Catchup rubber-band quietly trims engine output; Respotting snaps stragglers and wrecks back into the action; and Teleporting is the last-resort tool for keeping cars on a valid route. In Season mode a separate DCB (Dynamic difficulty Balance) tunes per-player skill over time. Everything is plaintext .params, so the numbers below are quoted directly from the shipped devkit files.

Difficulty modifier presets

GameLogic/DifficultyModifiers/ contains six preset files, each a /SSAI/DifficultySetting block selected per race via the race-setup field /RaceSetup/AI → 'Difficulty parameters file' = '001Default':

FileRole
000Hardest.paramsMaxed pack — every chance forced, full powerbar gain
001Default.paramsStandard single-player race director
002Hardest.paramsAlternate hardest variant (per-player skill + speed/slide curves)
Champ.paramsChampionship racing tuning
Demolition.paramsDemolition mode AI tuning
Elimination.paramsElimination mode — front-loaded grid spacing

Grid spacing ("Drivers")

The Drivers section is the most visible rubber-band: a per-grid-slot Initial Distance / Final Distance pair (metres relative to the racing line / player) that the director lerps between over the race. In 001Default the back markers start ahead and are reeled in toward the player by the finish — keeping the pack visually bunched: 

/SSAI/DifficultySetting/Drivers:    (001Default)
	Player 0  Initial 0      Final 0
	Player 2  Initial 50     Final -275
	Player 4  Initial 150    Final -125
	Player 6  Initial 250    Final 25
	Player 7  Initial 300    Final 100

The extremes show the design intent. 000Hardest pins every slot at Initial = Final = 1000 (the whole field permanently 1 km ahead — i.e. ruthless), 002Hardest uses 10000 with an explicit per-player Skill Factor = 1.0, and Elimination front-loads the leaders far ahead (Player 1 Initial 400 → Final 100) while letting the tail drift back, so the bubble constantly churns.

Skill behaviour at the extremes

Each preset describes what an AI does at skill 0 versus skill 1, blended by the pilot's DriverSkill and the live race director. The most telling block is the speed multiplier curve and the skill-0 handicaps. From 001Default: 

/SSAI/DifficultySetting/Driving:
	'000;Speed multiplier' = [ 0.6 0.65 0.7 0.8 1.0 1.0 1.0 1.03 1.06 1.1 ];
/SSAI/DifficultySetting/InternalParameters:
	'Extra compensation when Skill == 0'              = 0.5;
	'Look Ahead Multiplier when Skill == 0'           = 0.7;
	'Maximum width percentage to offset from Racing Line' = 0.75;
	'Speed Multiplier when Skill == 0'                = 2.0;

The 10-entry Speed multiplier array is a curve sampled by relative position: cars far behind get up to 1.1× speed, cars out front get throttled to 0.6×. A low-skill AI also gets a wider allowed racing-line offset (wanders more), shorter look-ahead (reacts late), and a larger speed multiplier — i.e. it is faster but sloppier, which reads as a believable weaker driver rather than an obviously slow one. Across presets the speed curve is shaped per mode:

PresetSpeed multiplier curve (10 samples)
001Default / Champ0.6 · 0.65 · 0.7 · 0.8 · 1.0 · 1.0 · 1.0 · 1.03 · 1.06 · 1.1
000Hardest / 002Hardest1.0 × 10 (flat — no handicap)
Demolition0.45 · 0.5 · 0.6 · 0.7 · 0.8 · 0.9 · 1.0 · 1.1 · 1.2 · 1.3
Elimination0.5 · 0.6 · 0.7 · 0.8 · 0.9 · 1.0 · 1.0 · 1.0 · 1.05 · 1.1

002Hardest additionally carries skill-vs-curve arrays the others omit, e.g. a Sliding compensation vs Skill ramp that makes high-skill cars slide far less: 

'007;Sliding compensation vs Skill' =
	[ 2.0 1.7 1.4 1.2 0.9 0.7 0.55 0.475 0.375 0.4 ];

Powerplay aggression by skill

The Powerplays / Skills section is where the director decides how often AI fires Powerplays, shortcuts and route-changers — and how the power bar fills. 001Default exposes a full skill-0/skill-1 matrix; key values:

Parameter (001Default)Skill 0Skill 1
Shortcut chances0.00.2
Powerplay chances on player0.00.3
Route Changer near player0.10.0
Route Changer when player not near0.00.2
Cooldown after any AI Powerplay (s)4.00.5
AI powerbar gain multiplier1.01.5

So a "skilled" AI charges its bar 1.5× faster and is far more willing to drop hazards on the player, while a weak one mostly fires harmless route-changers near itself. 000Hardest turns the dials to maximum — Min/Max Shortcut chances = 1.0, Min/Max Powerplay chances = 1.0, AI powerbar gain multiplier = 2.0 at both ends — meaning a hazard on virtually every opportunity. Global per-lap modifiers sit alongside: 

/SSAI/DifficultySetting/GlobalParameters:    (001Default)
	'001;Chances of using a shortcut … per lap'              = 0.1;
	'002;Chances of triggering a Powerplay on the player …'  = 0.18;
	'003;Chances of triggering a Powerplay on the AI …'      = 0.12;
	'004;Chances of triggering a Route Changer near player'  = 0.1;
	'005;Chances of triggering a Route Changer far …'        = 0.05;
/SSAI/DifficultySetting/RaceScript:
	'000;Time to set the AI players a skill of 1 …'          = 6.0;

That last line is a classic cinematic touch: for the first 6 seconds of a race the whole field is forced to skill 1, so the pack launches cleanly and dramatically off the line. The engine also supports faking race results entirely — SSAI/RaceScript/10;Fake race results according to race script, with min/max distances to separate players when doing so.

Catchup (rubber-band)

GameLogic/Catchup.params is a small, separate engine-level rubber-band that trims a trailing car's drivetrain rather than its target speed. In the shipped devkit data it is disabled — every value is zero — but the structure documents exactly what it can do: 

/Prototype/Catchup:
	'Distance from back for full penalty' = 0.0 (0.0, 5000.0);
	'Max torque gain'                     = 0.0 (0.0, 1.0);
	'Max torque loss'                     = 0.0 (0.0, 1.0);
	'Top speed gain'                      = 0.0 (0.0, 100.0);
FieldEffect
Distance from back for full penaltyHow far behind a car must be before the full effect applies (scaled by distance up to 5000 m)
Max torque gainEngine torque bonus given to a car that is behind
Max torque lossTorque penalty applied to a car that is ahead
Top speed gainExtra top speed (mph) granted to a trailing car
Two rubber-bands. Catchup is the physics-level band (torque / top speed). The DifficultySetting "Drivers" distances and "Speed multiplier" curve are the AI-level band. Because Catchup ships at all-zero, Split/Second's pack-tightening on the default profile comes from the AI-level director and from respotting, not from secretly boosting a trailing car's engine. Theory: the all-zero shipping values plus the /Prototype/ path suggest Catchup was an experimental feature largely superseded by the difficulty director.

Respotting

Respotting is how Split/Second recovers a car that has wrecked, fallen too far behind, gone the wrong way, or been left off a valid route — it teleports the car back to a sensible spot on the track and resumes it at speed. The engine driver is InGame::cRespotController (EBOOT: cRespotController::DoUpdate - player %i respot). Top-level tuning is in GameplayParameters.params: 

/GameplayParameters/Respotting:
	'000;Max distance for respotting on correct route from route changer' = 500.0;
	'001;Max delay when being respotted ahead due to obstacles, etc'      = 3.0;
	'002;Max distance to scale delay when respotting ahead'               = 100.0;

The bulk of the behaviour, though, lives in RaceSetupStatic.params under Logic/Respot — a surprisingly rich set of rules whose whole purpose is to make respots invisible to the camera. Key blocks:

Sub-systemNotable values
Delay (visibility)AI "Pop In/Pop Out Prevention" both on; max non-human respot delay 5.0 s; +2.0 s extra for visibility
NearPlayerDelayMax delay 3.0 s; min time-to-player 0.75 s; near-player arc half-angle 30°
TrackCurvatureCurvatureVsSpeed = [30 27 23 19 13 9 6 4 3 3]; look-ahead weights at 10/50/100 m
VelocityDistance to respot at maximum speed: 100 m
Wrong-way respotTime before respot: 2.0 s

The "Pop In / Pop Out Prevention" system delays a respot until the spot is off-camera, and the Victim of Wreckage Condition (max 2.5 s) keeps a wrecked AI visibly crashed for a beat before snapping it back. The EBOOT shows the controller logging MoveRespotForwardForFairness() — when an AI is respotted ahead it deliberately loses time so it doesn't gain an unfair advantage: 

MoveRespotForwardForFairness() - We need to lose time, approximately
	%.1f seconds, distance already forward = %.1f

The full respot transform (RespotPosX/Y/Z, RespotLookX/Y/Z, RespotSpeed, SpreadDistanceWidth/Length) is exposed on the QA debug menu under Respot/…, with a Force Respot cheat and a RespotTest page. The same respot machinery is what the EvolutionConfig harness uses to place test cars ("Respot speed proportion to the max speed of the car").

Respotting can fail. The EBOOT carries guards like WARNING : trying to respot car outside of track and Respot position … has no collision … trying a previous node — if the chosen node has no ground, the controller walks back along the route to find a valid one.

Teleporting

Teleporting is the harder cousin of respotting, used when a car is on a now-closed route or has wandered impossibly far from the scripted line. It is configured in GameplayParameters.params, and in the shipped data both conditional teleport modes are disabled by default: 

/GameplayParameters/Teleporting:
	'000;Teleport distance behind the player' = 100.0;
	'001;Teleport distance ahead the player'  = 200.0;

/GameplayParameters/Teleporting/Closed Routes:
	'000;Enable' = false;
	'001;Time limit to teleport'        = 2.5;
	'002;Distance limit to opened route' = 100.0;

/GameplayParameters/Teleporting/Far from scripted distance:
	'000;Enable' = false;
	'001;Time limit to teleport'         = 2.5;
	'002;Offset distance to perform teleport' = 1000.0;
	'003;Player distance check'          = 1000.0;
ModeTriggerWindowShipped
Behind / ahead windowAlways — bounds where a teleport may place a car100 m back / 200 m aheadactive
Closed RoutesCar stuck on a route that has closed2.5 s / within 100 m of an open routedisabled
Far from scripted distanceCar > 1000 m off the scripted line2.5 sdisabled

With the conditional modes off, default races lean on respotting (which is gentler and camera-aware) and only fall through to teleport bounds in extreme cases. The 100 m-behind / 200 m-ahead window is the same "keep the pack on screen" budget the difficulty Drivers spacing works within.

Season-mode DCB (Dynamic Balance)

Separate from the per-race presets, the campaign uses a DCB ("Dynamic difficulty Balance") value that adapts to how the player performs over a Season. The EBOOT exposes a Season Mode DCB Updater and a HUD debug page, and the static ranges live in RaceSetupStatic.params: 

/RaceSetupStatic/DCB/AI Skill:
	'01;Range Min Limit' = 0.3 (0.0, 1.0);
	'02;Range Max Limit' = 0.3 (0.0, 1.0);
/RaceSetupStatic/DCB/Difficulty Choice:
	'01;Easy'   = 0.2;
	'02;Medium' = 0.5;
	'03;Hard'   = 0.8;

The player's DCB is a 0–1 number combining a base player profile with per-event, per-track and per-vehicle modifiers (the EBOOT prints Player DCB = %.3f, Event DCB Modifier, Track DCB Modifier, Vehicle DCB Modifier). After each event the updater nudges it by leaderboard position (Leaderboard position: %d, DCB increment: %.3f) and maps it onto AI skill and onto a target score for the medal thresholds (per-mode Score at worst DCB / Score Range for Time Trial, Demolition, Nemesis Truck, Chopper Revenge/Survival). The Difficulty Choice values (0.2 / 0.5 / 0.8) are the Easy/Medium/Hard seed points the player selects.

The three difficulty layers stack: the player's DCB picks how skilled opponents are over the Season, the per-race DifficultyModifiers preset shapes spacing and Powerplay aggression within a race, and Respot/Teleport keep the resulting pack physically on screen. The physics-level Catchup band is available but off by default.
