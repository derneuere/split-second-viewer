Scoring & Progression

Split/Second wraps two related data-driven systems: a moment-to-moment scoring model (the Prototype/Scoring tuning group, served from Scoring/Scoring.params) that converts drifting, jumps, overtakes and high-speed driving into points and Powerplay charge, and a campaign progression system (the Season XML files) that lays out the 24-episode career, its difficulty ramp and the car-unlock economy. Both are plaintext on this devkit build, so the structure is fully readable; only the live tuning values in Scoring.params have been zeroed.

The scoring model

Scoring is owned by cScoringManager (with paired cScoringManagerInput / cScoringManagerOutput structures visible in the eboot). The runtime reads its tuning from Scoring/Scoring.params, whose header declares the directory /Prototype/Scoring. There are three sub-groups — Overtake, Speeding and the root Scoring group — plus per-vehicle drift/jump behaviour that lives in the AI LDC tables.

Devkit caveat. Every right-hand value in this build's Scoring.params is 0.0; only the (min, max) slider ranges survive. That is normal for a QA build whose live tuning ships compiled into the global registry / save data. The fields and their ranges are therefore Confirmed; the shipped values are Opaque here.

Root scoring group

The root /Prototype/Scoring group sets the per-second rates for the two skill events (drift and jump), the combo system, and the conversion between raw score and Powerplay charge. The "best score" and "default … to beat" entries are the per-event targets a player races against (the HUD shows BEST JUMP! / BEST DRIFT! when these are exceeded — see HUD & Frontend).

Overtake scoring

The /Prototype/Scoring/Overtake group scores positions taken from rivals. The base award (Score awarded for overtaking, 0–1000) is scaled by which place you took — there are seven per-position scales, Score scale for taking 1st … 7th (each 0.0–1.0), so a pass for 1st place can be worth a different amount than a pass for 7th. A Combo multiplier (0.0–2.0) stacks rapid overtakes, and a Delay time before next award (0.0–10.0 s) rate-limits the bonus. The matching debug-menu paths exist in the eboot (Prototype/Scoring/Overtake/Score scale for taking 1st … 7th).

Speeding (high-speed) scoring

A tiny /Prototype/Scoring/Speeding group rewards sustained speed: above a Min car speed required (0.0–500.0, in the game's speed units) the player earns Score awarded per second (0.0–1000.0). This is the points side of the same "driving fast" behaviour that also charges the AI power bar and triggers the High_Speed post-process.

The eboot also exposes RaceSetup/Scoring/Disable Drifting Hud Events and RaceSetup/Scoring/PilotEpisode/PilotEpisodeScore, and a CameraDirector/CameraConditions/Use Drifting Or Jumping Condition hook — drifting/jumping state feeds the camera director as well as the score.

Score → Powerplay charge

Score is the currency that fills the power bar. The root group's Score required for powerplay sets the per-level threshold, Points per Powerplay the reward for spending it, and Default power to score ratio the baseline conversion. The actual bar mechanics, danger zones and trigger logic live in the Powerplay System; scoring just supplies the fuel. The HUD's power bar (driven by DialsPower.gfx) renders this charge — see HUD & Frontend.

Season campaign (Season.xml)

Season/Season.xml defines the single-player career as a flat list of <Episode> elements (the in-game "season" framed as a reality-TV show). This build contains 24 episodes, each with four or five <Event> entries and one <UnlockableCar>. Episodes 1–24 form the spine of the track rotation.

Episode attributes

AttributeTypeExampleDescription
Numberint1 … 24Episode index / career order
ScoreToUnlockFinaleint16Star/score total needed to open the episode finale (constant 16 across all episodes here)
VehicleSkillLimitfloat0.2 → 1.0Caps how high-performance a car the player may bring; ramps with progress

Each <Event> carries GameMode, Track, SubTrack (A–D), Variation (1 or 2) and a DCBModifier. The DCB modifier ("Dynamic Catch-up / Balance") is the per-event difficulty knob — it climbs almost monotonically from 0.5 in Episode 1 to 1.0 by the late episodes, which is how the campaign tightens the rubber-band AI as you progress. The VehicleSkillLimit steps in lockstep: 0.2 (eps 1–3), 0.4 (4–8), 0.6 (9–14), 0.8 (15–19) and 1.0 (20–24).

</Episode>

The game modes referenced across the season are QuickRace, TimeTrial, Elimination, Demolition, Nemesis, HelicopterSurvival and HelicopterRevenge — exactly the modes documented on Game Modes and Nemesis & Heli Modes. Tracks used are Downtown, docks, airport_test_03, Graveyard, Powerplant and the Nemesis variants nem_graveyard, nem_warehouse, nem_downtown and nem_storm (see Tracks & Levels).

Season.xml's header comment documents three ways to restrict cars per episode: an exact <Car Name="Musclecar_01" />, a class filter <Car Type="Musclecar" /> (any car whose name starts with the type), or omit car entries entirely to allow all unlocked vehicles. The shipped episodes use the third form.

The <Free> list and <InitialCars> in Season.xml do not match exactly (Free adds Supercar_01 / Musclecar_04 / Musclecar_06 and drops Truck_03). This is a devkit/QA tuning state — the two files are edited independently — so treat the precise free-roster as Theory for the shipped game.

Online score upload

Scores also feed the online leaderboards. The eboot string cScoring::NetUpload() - Player %d sending new score = %d shows each player's cScoring::mScore is pushed over the Quazal/ATVNet duplicated-object layer; the frontend leaderboard movies (FE_leaderboards_*.gfx) then read it back. See HUD & Frontend for those Scaleform assets.
