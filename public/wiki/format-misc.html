Misc & Raw

A grab-bag of small or single-purpose container formats that don't warrant their own page. Most are thin big-endian "typed array" blobs (a count, a stride, then raw records); two carry their own ASCII magic. Each section below gives a one-line identification, the evidence behind it, and a real hex peek. Confidence is per-format.

.data — particle / timeline blob Theory

Found throughout Environments/Levels/<Level>/Particles/TimelineParticles/… and Subtracks/<X>/Particles/… (91 files, 44 B – 133 KB). A big-endian typed-array container: a leading count and element size, then raw records (floats/zeros). The 44-byte Flare.data is byte-identical across 9 of the 10 levels — a constant template stub (the airport_test_03 copy is a larger 624-byte variant) — while real per-track particle data reaches 130 KB. Paired with the emitter / Catnip timeline system. 

Flare.data (44 bytes):
000000  00 00 00 01  00 00 00 10  00 00 00 00  00 00 00 00
        └ count=1    └ stride=16  └──── record (zeros) ────
000010  00 00 00 00  00 00 00 10  00 00 00 00  00 00 00 00
000020  00 00 00 01  00 00 00 04  00 00 00 00

.rdata — vehicle handling config Confirmed

42 files total. 39 live under Vehicles/Handling/Configs/ named per car (Musclecar_01.rdata, Supercar_…, …) and are each 56 bytes; the other 3 are 204-byte files under Vehicles/Bodies/ for the Nemesis truck and its trailers (NemesisTruck01.rdata, NemTruck1Trai1/2.rdata). The EBOOT builds the handling path with %s%s.rdata. The 56-byte handling body is a big-endian header (count=1, n=4) followed by twelve float32 values that group into four mirrored vec3 points — the per-wheel contact/handling points. Symmetry (±X with matching Y/Z) is the tell. The 204-byte truck files share the leading count=1 word but carry a larger second field (n=10) and many more floats (extra wheel/contact points for the multi-axle truck + trailers), so the structure scales by n rather than being a fixed 56 bytes. 

Musclecar_01.rdata (56 bytes):
000000  00 00 00 01  00 00 00 04  3f 5b bb 01  3e b1 f1 eb
        └ count=1    └ n=4        └ 0.8583     └ 0.3475   (vec3 #0 x,y…)
000010  3f a9 08 3f  bf 5b d6 5b  3e b1 eb 52  3f a9 06 9f
                     └ -0.8587    (vec3 #1 — mirror of #0 in X)
  → 4 points:  (+0.858, 0.347, 1.321)  (-0.859, 0.347, 1.321)
               (+0.920, 0.369,-1.307)  (-0.922, 0.369,-1.307)   front/rear wheels

See Vehicle Physics (.phys) and Vehicles for how handling data is consumed.

.bin — audio index & replay log Partial

Only 3 .bin files, all under Audio/, of two distinct kinds:

  • Audio/Swooshes/Swooshes.bin (12 B) — a tiny big-endian index for the "swoosh" whoosh SFX (UpdateSwooshes, SideChainSwooshes in the EBOOT; localised lists in …_Audio_Swooshes.xml).
  • Audio/Replay/audio_commands_recording*.bin (5–6 MB) — a FourCC-tagged stream of recorded audio commands for replay/QA. A one-time header (vers=3, type=0) is followed by a long run of records, each a 4-char ASCII tag (time, cmd , dtime) plus a big-endian payload. The cmd  tag appears 21,034× across this 5.0 MB recording (12× in the first 4 KB, 221× in the first 64 KB) — one per recorded audio command. The companion audio_commands_recording_finish.bin is 5.9 MB.
audio_commands_recording.bin (head):
000000  76 65 72 73  00 00 00 03  74 79 70 65  00 00 00 00
        └ 'vers'      =3          └ 'type'      =0
000010  74 69 6d 65  00 00 05 e0  63 6d 64 20  00 00 00 08
        └ 'time'      =0x5E0      └ 'cmd '      len=8
000020  00 00 00 1f  82 08 84 64  74 69 6d 65  00 00 05 e1
        └ command payload …       └ 'dtime' (delta-time) …
Swooshes.bin (12 bytes):
000000  00 00 00 01  00 00 00 02  00 00 00 00

.dat — powerplay info table Partial

10 files, all <Level>_powerplayInfo.dat (EBOOT: _powerplayInfo.dat), a per-level table for the powerplay system. Size is not fixed. The file opens with a big-endian uint32 record count, then that many records, each holding a hash, a length-prefixed .cat entity name (e.g. pp12_road_explode_mpo.cat, pg17_silo_mpr.cat) and a block of transform floats — i.e. the placed powerplay instances for the level. The five main race levels carry full tables (3.7 KB – 7.8 KB); four nem_* levels are empty 4-byte stubs (count = 0) and nem_training is a small 166-byte table. Documented in full on its own page: Powerplay Info (.dat). 

Downtown_powerplayInfo.dat (7,839 bytes) head:
000000  00 00 00 58  80 73 33 3c  00 00 00 19  70 70 31 32
        └ count=0x58 └ hash       └ nameLen=25 └ "pp12…"
000010  5f 72 6f 61  64 5f 65 78  70 6c 6f 64  65 5f 6d 70   "_road_explode_mp"
000020  6f 2e 63 61  74 …                                    "o.cat" + transform floats →

nem_downtown_powerplayInfo.dat (4 bytes):
000000  00 00 00 00     ← record count = 0 (empty stub)

.ker — font kerning table Partial

A single file, fonts/Debug.ker (4,252 B), the kerning data for the debug font. The EBOOT prints KerningCount = %d and references kerning, confirming the role. Big-endian header: a leading count (0x40 = 64), a second count (0x2A = 42), then pairs of float32 kerning offsets, followed by a glyph-index map (the ascending byte run 00 01 02 03 …). 

Debug.ker (head):
000000  00 00 00 40  2a 00 00 00  29 d0 cc 3b  29 d0 4c 3b
        └ count=64   └ 42         └ float32 kerning offsets …
000010  ab aa aa 3d  00 00 00 40  00 00 00 00  00 01 02 03
                                               └ glyph index map →

.bed — font bitmap descriptor Theory

A single file, fonts/Debug.bed (4,986 B). It opens with a length-prefixed ASCII texture name, debug.tga, then per-glyph records (positions/advances). It is the bitmap glyph atlas descriptor that pairs the .tga font sheet with the .ker kerning table — together they define the debug overlay font. 

Debug.bed (head):
000000  01 01 64 65  62 75 67 2e  74 67 61 00  00 01 80 00
        └ ?  └ 'd''e''b''u''g''.''t''g''a' \0  └ glyph records →
000010  be 00 04 00  00 00 00 00  2d 31 01 00  42 0f 00 00

.dct — localisation dictionary Confirmed (header)

16 files under Dictionary/, one pair per language/platform (ENGLISH_PS3.dct, ENGLISH_XBOX360.dct, DUTCH_PS3.dct, …). The EBOOT loads them with %s/%s_%s.dct and LoadDictionary: %s. These carry an explicit ASCII magic DICT and are little-endian (the one mis-endian format in this cluster besides .gfx): a version word (0x2000), a constant field (19 in every file — likely a hash-bucket count or sub-version), an entry count at offset 0x10 that scales with content (142 for ENGLISH_PS3, 394 for GERMAN_PS3, 930 for StringIDs_PS3), a table of {nameHash, stringOffset, reserved} records, and a packed string blob at the tail (readable UI text — "NO AWARD", "3 laps of explosive racing!", "GIOTTO 512GT", … — all four verified present in the file). 

ENGLISH_PS3.dct (head):
000000  44 49 43 54  00 20 00 00  8f 77 e9 18  13 00 00 00
        └ 'D''I''C''T' └ ver=0x2000 LE  └ hash?       └ 0x13=19 (constant)
000010  8e 00 00 00  01 00 00 00  af 06 00 00  00 00 00 00
        └ entryCount=0x8E (142)       └ stringOffset≈0x6AF └ reserved
…
TAIL: …NO AWARD.BACK.3 laps of explosive racing!.GREAT JUMP.…GIOTTO 512GT.…
The entry count at 0x10 (142 here) is verified — it tracks the size of each dictionary, and the field at 0x0C is a constant 19 across all 16 files. The record table reads as little-endian {hash:4, offset:4, reserved:4} and the readable string blob sits at the tail (region begins near 0x6C9 in this file). The exact record start offset and the base the stringOffset field is measured from did not resolve cleanly to string boundaries in testing, so hash-to-string resolution stays unverified. Exact hash-to-string resolution is not yet byte-verified, hence the header is Confirmed but the record math is Partial.

.controllerTemplate — FX controller template Theory

A single file, Vehicles/Particles/Standard/VehicleParticles.controllerTemplate (6,236 B), addressed from the Resident archive in the EBOOT (Resident|Vehicles/Particles/Standard/VehicleParticles.controllerTemplate). Big-endian header (count=5, len=0x16) then fixed-width slots, each holding a numeric ID string and a named controller (e.g. CollisionSparks). It's a Catnip-style template table that maps named particle controllers to their parameters for vehicle FX. 

VehicleParticles.controllerTemplate (head):
000000  00 00 00 05  00 00 00 16  31 32 35 30  30 37 33 37
        └ count=5    └ len=0x16   └ "12500737…"  (controller ID string)
000010  36 37 2e 34  38 39 32 36  36 37 34 32  00 00 00 00
        … "67.489266742"\0
000030  43 6f 6c 6c  69 73 69 6f  6e 53 70 61  72 6b 73 00
        └ "CollisionSparks"\0   (named controller)

Open questions

  • .data: decode the record stride/type tag and tie it to the emitter system; only the count/stride header is solid.
  • .ker/.bed: confirm the glyph-record stride and the float-pair semantics (kerning in twips vs normalised units).
  • .dct: verify the hash function and confirm the offset field resolves against the string blob exactly.
  • .bin replay: enumerate the full FourCC command vocabulary and payload widths.
